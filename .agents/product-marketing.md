## Product Overview
**One-liner:** A GTM leader who is also an AI operator — proven revenue leadership plus a real, governed, production AI agent fleet he runs himself.
**What it does:** Replaces a traditional resume with a full portfolio site: home page covering three pillars (GTM Leadership, Agentic Systems, Products Built), each linking to a deep-dive page, plus a compact career timeline and a downloadable ATS-friendly PDF resume.
**Product category:** Executive/GTM-leadership candidate positioning site, for AI-native companies specifically.
**Product type:** Personal portfolio site (static, no build step), not a company or SaaS product.
**Business model:** N/A — the "conversion" is a hiring conversation, not a purchase.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** AI labs and high-growth AI-native startups building or scaling a go-to-market motion.
**Decision-makers:** Founders/CEOs, CROs/Heads of Revenue, VP Sales, board members reviewing exec hires, and technical recruiters screening for GTM leadership roles.
**Primary use case:** Evaluating Patrick as a GTM leadership hire — specifically whether he has both the revenue-leadership track record AND real technical fluency with agentic/AI-native operating models (not just "used AI" resume-inflation).
**Jobs to be done:**
- Verify a real, numbers-backed revenue/GTM leadership track record (not just titles)
- Verify the "AI operator" claim is real, not resume padding (governs a production agent fleet, diagnoses problems and directs the fix while agents write the code, real CI/security discipline)
- Get a read on judgment, communication style, and voice directly from the writing, since this site IS a work sample
**Use cases:**
- A hiring manager sourced via LinkedIn clicks through to vet before a screening call
- A recruiter shares the link internally as the "here's proof" artifact ahead of a panel round

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Hiring leaders at AI-native companies need a GTM leader who can operate at the intersection of revenue leadership and technical/agentic systems, and that combination is rare.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Traditional GTM/CRO candidates: strong revenue track record, but no real technical practice, "AI-native" is a buzzword on their resume, not something they've built
- Technical/ops leaders: real technical chops, but no GTM leadership pedigree or revenue ownership
**What it costs them:** A mis-hire at the GTM leadership level is expensive and slow to unwind, especially at a company whose whole operating model depends on AI-native execution.
**Emotional tension:** Skepticism toward "AI-native" as a claim (it's overused and easy to fake); wanting proof, not a pitch.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other CRO/Head-of-Revenue candidates with a similar staffing/services background — fall short because they don't have a real, governed technical practice to point to, just a claim of "being comfortable with AI tools."
**Secondary:** Technical/ops leaders with real AI-systems chops — fall short because they haven't led go-to-market, built revenue lines, or owned a P&L.
**Indirect:** An internal promote or a generalist "growth" hire — falls short on either the GTM discipline or the technical depth, usually both.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Real revenue outcomes with real numbers (122% YoY growth, 2.7× a quarter vs. a prior year, 21.7× EBITDA growth, 4 business lines built from 1)
- A real, production, cost-governed, security-reviewed multi-agent fleet he directs, not codes himself — he diagnoses problems and gives direction, his agents (Emma, principal engineer agent) write and ship the code — not a claim, a public repo (severs-agent-shared) and named specific fixes back it up
- Recent (last 3 years), not dated — the AI-native chapter is his most current and most relevant experience
**How we do it differently:** Most candidates pick one lane (GTM or technical). This site's entire structure (three pillars, each with a deep-dive) is built to prove both are real and connected — the agent fleet is what made him efficient as a GTM operator, not a side hobby.
**Why that's better:** A hiring leader gets verifiable proof instead of a claim, on both halves of the hybrid pitch.
**Why customers choose us:** Specificity everywhere — named numbers, named people (with real testimonials), a real public repo, real named enterprise clients — instead of generic "results-driven leader" language.

**Anti-persona:** Companies looking for a purely technical IC hire (not a leadership role), companies wanting someone to run a traditional/legacy GTM motion untouched by agentic tooling, or non-AI-native businesses where the hybrid pitch doesn't resonate.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Left G2i (as of July 2026); ambition for a bigger stage — an AI lab or high-growth AI-native startup rather than another staffing-adjacent business.
**Pull:** Proof of the hybrid GTM + technical-operator capability, real revenue outcomes already delivered, a real production system already in place and governed.
**Habit:** Hiring leaders default to hiring "pure GTM" or "pure technical" leaders because the hybrid profile is unfamiliar and harder to evaluate.
**Anxiety:** Whether the technical side is genuine given he's not a formally trained engineer; whether 3 years of the AI-native chapter is enough given a longer prior career in more conventional sales/revenue leadership (25 years total).
